Boiled hotdogs

Take me back!

Simple food that carries the complex flavors

"Fine Dining". I personally have only heard that term from academics, rich people and the media. No one I trust have been to a Fine Dining. How do I know that it acutally exists? Where's the evidence? I'm not saying that it's a conspiracy, but the goverment sure does seem to make it so that no ordinary man visits these "Fine Diners". I've driven past a few during my nigthly rides in my Volvo. Everyone is empty. A bit strange, right?

Anyways, the working class at the bottom of the pyramid (a.k.a. all of us) still have good food to eat. Like, who doesn't love the a good, steaming hot dog straight from the kettle above a huge bonfire? Yeah, sure it's not a complex Sóúp dè Flúffë or whatever the upper class claims that they eat. But boiled meat, bread, ketchup, mustard and bit of roasted onions? Oof, if that doesn't pack a taste punch, I don't want to eat ever again.

Enough reading, let's get to the good stuff.

Ingredients

  • 1 can of "Bullens Pilsnerkorv" - often found at the exotic food shelf
  • A couple of bread buns
  • Heinz tomato ketchup
  • Mustard (the Swedish brand "Slotts" is really good)
  • Roasted onions for serving

Cooking time

  1. Start a bonfire.
  2. Make the fire BIGGER.
  3. Wait for the fire to settle down.
  4. Get a bit impatient and place the can of Bullens Pilsnerkorv amidst the flames.
  5. Wait for it to get to a raging boil.
  6. Take the can out of the fire. Make sure to burn your hands, but act like you didn't feel a thing.
  7. Put the sausage in a bun.
  8. Add condiments and garnish after your liking.
