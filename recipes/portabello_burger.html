Vegan Burger Bliss

Who hasn't been in the mood for a healthy, nutritios meal? I know I haven't. I wouldn't cook this vegan food voluntarily, but since my daughters boyfriend refuses to even look at cutting board that has been used to cut meat on, I haven't had any choice. Burgers does at least have a bit of inherent masculinity, so that makes things a bit easier for me. I'm very lucky that I can write these recipes anonymously, because I would lose the respect of my co-workers at the Volvo factory if they knew I made a meal where at least something hasn't died.

Anyways, take a big swig of strong whisky or smoke a cigar. You need to up your manlihood by a few points, because this meal will debuff those stats. I usually start my spotify playlist with a lot of guy music, crank up the volume so I can drown out the Hyperpop from my daughters room. Having this playlist going I can blame my tears on the hardcore riffs and awesome lyrics, instead of the fact that I'm cooking mushrooms.

Ingredients

  • 1 Portobello mushroom, preferebly less
  • Rucolla or some other kind of green
  • Burger buns
  • Some vegan condiment, like ketchup

Cooking time

  1. Fry the mushroom on soaring heat.
  2. Add mushroom to burger bun.
  3. Add ketchup and rucolla
  4. Make sure that none of your tears fall onto the burger. That wouldn't be vegan
  5. If he complains about the taste, well it's not so surprising, since the meal contains absolutely zero muscle fibers.
  6. Try to convince your daughter that your ex-wife does, in fact, not have to come pick her up, and that you do in fact act as any adult and mature father would.
